5 Things You Need to Know About Linen

October 29, 2024

Linen has been a go-to fabric for centuries, admired for its breathability, durability, and natural charm. Whether you’re new to linen or have worn it for years, here are five key things you should know about this timeless fabric:

1. It’s One of the Oldest Fabrics in the World

Linen’s history dates back thousands of years. Ancient Egyptians valued it for its purity and used it in their most sacred rituals, including mummification. Some of the earliest linen clothing fragments were discovered in prehistoric caves, proving that linen has been used for both practical and ceremonial purposes for millennia. Owning a piece of linen clothing is like holding a piece of history. It’s one of the most durable natural fabrics, and it gets better with time.

2. Linen is Extremely Breathable

New linen fabric may feel stiff, but don’t let that deter you! One of linen’s magical qualities is that it softens the more it’s worn and washed. Over time, the fibers relax and become incredibly smooth and soft to the touch, making your linen clothing even more enjoyable. Linen gets better with age, so your favorite linen shirt or pants will feel better and fit more naturally the more you wear them.

3. It Softens with Time

New linen fabric may feel stiff, but don’t let that deter you! One of linen’s magical qualities is that it softens the more it’s worn and washed. Over time, the fibers relax and become incredibly smooth and soft to the touch, making your linen clothing even more enjoyable. Linen gets better with age, so your favorite linen shirt or pants will feel better and fit more naturally the more you wear them.

4. It’s Environmentally Friendly

Linen is one of the most sustainable fabrics you can choose. The flax plant requires minimal water and pesticides compared to cotton, and every part of the plant can be used, resulting in less waste. Plus, linen is biodegradable, which means when your linen clothes eventually wear out, they won’t sit in a landfill for centuries. Choosing linen means making an environmentally conscious decision that supports sustainability in fashion.

5. Linen is Wrinkle-Prone—And That’s Okay!

Linen is known for its natural texture, which includes wrinkles. While some people view this as a downside, the wrinkles give linen its charm and casual, lived-in look. You can iron it if you prefer a crisp appearance, but many embrace the relaxed aesthetic that comes with the fabric’s crinkles. Embrace the effortless elegance of linen. The wrinkles are part of its character, making it perfect for casual, chic outfits.
